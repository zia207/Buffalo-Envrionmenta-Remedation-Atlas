Chemical inventory expanded categories · research export

    
Every site and facility in the atlas is linked to a broader chemical inventory beyond the most frequently reported legacy contaminants.
      Categories include PFAS, brominated flame retardants (BFR), pesticides, and pharmaceuticals & personal care products (PPCPs),
      inferred from EPA NPL records, DEC program and site typology (including NYSDEC's requirement to sample PFAS and 1,4-dioxane at all Part 375 remediation sites), TRI chemical identity, and emerging-contaminant screening literature. Each exported row carries the site's ZIP code for neighborhood-level cross-referencing. These are screening-level associations, not confirmatory sampling results.

About the data what each layer is, and where it comes from

    

      This atlas overlays five public datasets covering the eight-county Western New York region (Erie, Niagara, Cattaraugus, Chautauqua, Genesee, Wyoming, Orleans and Allegany). Records are point locations except the county and ZIP layers, which are boundaries. Coordinates from the New York State databases were converted from their native UTM Zone 18N (NAD83) projection to latitude/longitude for web mapping.
    

    

      

        
Federal Superfund (NPL)6

        
U.S. EPA · CERCLA National Priorities List

        
The most seriously contaminated sites in the country, cleaned up under the federal Superfund law. The Western New York set is dominated by the Niagara Falls chemical-landfill cluster — including Love Canal, the site that prompted the 1980 Superfund law — plus Eighteen Mile Creek in Lockport and Wide Beach in Brant. Each carries contaminants of concern, listing year, and cleanup status.

        
Coverage: WNY region · Status field: active cleanup, construction complete, or deleted from the NPL

        Source: EPA Superfund Site Profiles & Cleanups in My Community — map22.epa.gov/cimc

      


      

        
NYS DEC cleanup sites941

        
NYSDEC · Environmental Remediation Databases

        
Every state-tracked remediation site in the region, spanning five programs: State Superfund (468), Brownfield Cleanup (338), Environmental Restoration (54), Voluntary Cleanup (46) and RCRA corrective action (34). Each site carries a DEC site-class code (1–5, A, C, N, P) describing whether it poses a significant threat, is being actively remediated, or is closed. This layer is the map equivalent of DEC's DER External Search and also captures the brownfields shown in EPA's CIMC.

        
Coverage: all 8 WNY counties · Vintage: DEC Remedial GIS extract

        Source: DEC DER External Search — appfactory.dec.ny.gov/DERExternalSearch

      


      

        
Chemical Exposure Action Map16

        
Environmental Defense Fund (EDF) · CEAM

        
Industrial facilities that reported toxic chemical releases to EPA's Toxics Release Inventory, screened by EDF for health risk. Each record gives the reported chemical, whether it is a PFAS compound, five-year release totals, and how much of that total is linked to cancer, developmental, or respiratory harm — alongside the demographics of the population within 10 km, supporting an environmental-justice reading. A dedicated panel above lets you sort and export these facilities.

        
Coverage: WNY facilities reporting to TRI · Window: 5-year release sums

        Source: EDF Chemical Exposure Action Map — ceam.edf.org

      


      

        
Chemical Bulk Storage84

        
NYSDEC · Bulk Storage GIS

        
Active Chemical Bulk Storage (CBS) facilities in Erie and Niagara counties — stationary tanks holding hazardous chemicals above regulatory thresholds. Unlike the cleanup layers, these are not known contamination events; they flag where regulated chemical inventories sit within the community today. Petroleum bulk storage (mostly gas stations) is excluded to keep the map focused on higher-hazard chemical facilities.

        
Coverage: Erie & Niagara, active CBS only · Excludes: petroleum & closed facilities

        Source: NYSDEC Bulk Storage database (Chemical & Petroleum Bulk Storage GIS)

      


      

        
Boundary layers8 · 212

        
U.S. Census · reference geography

        
Two toggleable boundary overlays for spatial context: the eight WNY county outlines, and 212 ZIP-code areas (Census ZCTAs) covering the region. Turn on ZIP boundaries from the map-layer chips and hover any polygon to read its ZIP code — useful for cross-referencing a site against a mailing address or a neighborhood.

        
Counties: 8 · ZIP/ZCTA areas: 212 (simplified for web display)

        Source: U.S. Census TIGER/ZCTA via public GeoJSON (OpenDataDE, plotly datasets)

      


      

        
Social Vulnerability Index (SVI)ZCTA

        
CDC/ATSDR · Place and Health (GRASP)

        
The CDC/ATSDR Social Vulnerability Index ranks every New York ZCTA by relative vulnerability using census-derived themes and indicators — the same structure as the ATSDR SVI Interactive Map. Toggle SVI index and choose from Overall SVI, four theme ranks, or individual indicators (below poverty, unemployed, housing cost burden, disability, minority status, crowded housing, and more). Layer remediation sites against neighborhood vulnerability for environmental-justice context.

        
Geography: ZCTA · Comparison: NY State percentile ranks (2022)

        Source: CDC/ATSDR SVI · embedded WNY extract (2022 CDC/ATSDR SVI)

Program classifications and cleanup status change over time — confirm any specific site against the official DEC and EPA databases before relying on it. The NYC OER cleanup dataset referenced in some environmental-remediation catalogs covers New York City only and is not applicable to the Buffalo region, so it is not included here.
